\chapter{Good habits}
One major purpose of the essay is to give the student a way to acquire good working habits. Some of these are listed in this chapter.

\section{Logbook}
Make it a habit to always write your activities, results, files generated etc.\ in a logbook (digital or physical). This is very handy to have at the end when trying to summarize large amounts of experiments. Also, it is a very good thing to have should you need to prove the work is yours (if, for example, a collaborator would try to publish the findings without including you in the author list). Make sure to write dates in the logbook and to make daily entries in it. \cite{karplus1990molecular}

\section{Backup}
Any form of backup is better than no backup! (From there, you can start to optimize how you do your backups.) For example, a first backup strategy might be to send the material to yourself by email or copy it to a USB key. Make it a habit, starting today, to back up your source files (tex, py, etc.) when you are done working for the day. In this way, you will maximally lose one day of work if you computer crashes.

\section{Working hours}
You know at what times of the day you are efficient. However, it is not advisable to work all night. Since the essay period is several weeks long, you need to sleep, do sports, relax etc.\ to recover from the strain of working. Also, try to clearly distinguish work time from non-work time. This will help you to be as efficient as possible when working. If you work too much, you risk falling sick which will prevent you from working during several days.

\section{When you get stuck}
Do not panic! The world is unlikely to end just now. Remember you can consult your fellow students, supervisor, tutor(s), and Prof.\ Mama at agreed times.
